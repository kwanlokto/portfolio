# *The Phoenix Project*
**Gene Kim, Kevin Behr & George Spafford** · 2013
 
---
 
Often called *The Goal* for software engineering, this business novel follows an IT manager tasked with rescuing a failing software project. It's a narrative wrapper around DevOps and lean manufacturing principles — and it works surprisingly well.
 
The core lessons are hard to forget: optimise for flow, eliminate bottlenecks, and make work visible. The "Brent" character — a brilliant engineer who's also a single point of failure for everything — will feel uncomfortably familiar to most developers.
 
As someone who writes code, it reframed how I think about *shipping* software. A pull request is just one step in a value stream that stretches from business need to production. Understanding that whole pipeline makes you a better collaborator, not just a better coder.
 
The prose is functional at best, and the manufacturing metaphors can feel like a stretch. But the ideas are worth it.
 
**Rating: 4.5/5**
